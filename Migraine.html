Migraine



Migraine is a type of headache in which there are attacks of pain in the head, usually on one side, lasting from 4 to 72 hours, associated with nausea, vomiting, sensitivity to bright light and/or loud noise.

It may be triggered by certain precipitating situations (triggers) which can vary from individual to individual. Common triggers reported are food items (tea, ice cream, chocolate, chinese food), inadequate sleep, exposure to bright light, not having food on time, certain odours and stressors.

Headaches can be severe and can affect efficiency at work. While appropriate management of triggers is extremely important, frequent headaches may need medications that are proven to prevent migraines with varying degrees of efficacy.

Maintaining a headache diary can help the patient identify potential triggers. Keeping an eye on the screen time is vital as increased screen time may potentially lead to migraine attacks.
