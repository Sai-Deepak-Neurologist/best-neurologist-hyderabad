Seizures

Seizures are caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain. They can broadly be divided into two types, generalised seizures and focal onset seizures.

Generalised convulsive seizures are characterised by convulsive limb jerks with unresposiveness. Sometimes, it may lead to tongue bite due to convulsions of the jaw and tongue muscles. Involuntary passage of urine may also occur. On the other hand, focal onset seizures start in a specific part of the brain and may or maynot spread to the other regions.

Symptoms of focal seizures depend on the part where the seizure starts. Patients may be fully conscious in certain focal onset seizures. In focal motor seizures, twitching of a part of the body may be seen. Sensory seizures may manifest as abnormal sensations in a part of the body. Autonomic seizures may present as palpitations, other cardiac, gastro intestinal or urinary symptoms. Some focal seizures may present only as vertigo, behavioural abnormalities or fear.

An aura is a perception of an imminent seizure prior to losing consciousness. It usually occurs few seconds before the seizure in certain focal onset seizures. Commonly described aura include epigastric discomfort, fear, palpitations and vertigo.

Evaluating the cause of seizures usually entails brain scan, electroencephalography (EEG) and blood tests. Sometimes investigations may all be normal. Clinical description and videos of the episode may aid in diagnosis.

Treatment of seizures is with antiepileptics. Most patients have good seizure control with a single antiepileptic. A few may need multiple medications. A minority of them may be resistant to medication. Such patients may benefit by evaluating for any possible benefit from surgical treatment of epilepsy.
