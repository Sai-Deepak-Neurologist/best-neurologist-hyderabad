Meningoencephalitis


Meningoencephalitis refers to inflammation in the brain or its coverings. It can occur due to infection, tumours, chemicals and autoimmunity among other reasons. Patients may present with fever, headache, vomitings, neck pain, reduced consciousness, seizures, memory or behavioural issues and other symptoms depending on the region of the brain affected.

Infective meningitis can occur due to bacteria, virus, fungi and parasites. It can be life threatening. Prompt treatment with appropriate anti microbial is essential. Treatment of non infective meningitis depends on the specific cause, such as steroids may be needed in autoimmune illness, cancer chemotherapy in malignant meninigitis etc.

To establish a diagnosis of meningitis, investigations such as brain scan, lumbar puncture and CSF analysis may help. Lumbar puncture involves passing a needle into the space between the bones in the lower spine and collecting the fluid that covers the brain and spinal cord, cerebrospinal fluid (CSF).

In some instances, meningitis can cause complications and long term problems such as hydrocephalus, vision impairment, low sodium levels in blood and epilepsy. Some of the patients may also need neurosurgery in treating the complications as a life saving procedure.
