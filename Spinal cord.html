Spinal cord disorders


Spinal cord connects the limbs and other structures in the trunk to the brain. Problems in the cord can lead to paralysis, sensory abnormalities such as numbness and autonomic disturbances such as urinary issues among other symptoms.

Cord problems can occur due to pathologies outside the cord such as compression by disc, or diseases damaging the cord from within such as multiple sclerosis. Symptoms depend on the cause and location of the disease.

Evaluation usually involves MRI of the cord, followed by in appropriate cases CSF analysis and blood workup. Specific tests such as Vitamin B 12 levels in the blood and workup for auto immune disorders may be required if clinically appropriate.

Treatment depends on the cause. It may involve surgery in cases such as compression of the cord or oral or IV medications in situations such as autoimmunity and nutritional deficiencies.
