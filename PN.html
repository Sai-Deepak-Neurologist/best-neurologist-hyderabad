Peripheral Neuropathy

Peripheral neuropathy refers to disease of the nerves in the limbs. Broadly, nerves have fibres carrying motor, sensory and autonomic signals. Symptoms depend on the type of fibres affected.

Predominantly, motor neuropathy presents with weakness and thinning of limbs. Sensory neuropathy usually causes paresthesias like tingling, burning sensations, pins and needles sensation. Sometimes, it may lead to loss of sensation and imbalance. Autonomic neuropathy can present with abnormalities in sweating, heart rate, urinary disturbances, gastro intestinal and visual symptoms among other symptoms.

Causes of neuropathy can be multiple. Common causes include alcohol intake, nutritional deficiencies, Diabetes, cancer chemotherapeutic medications, and autoimmune diseases. Some neuropathies can have a genetic basis.

Evaluation entails nerve conduction studies in which small electric shocks are given to the limbs and sometimes in the neck to see the functioning of the nerves. Specific testing for finding the cause of illness may include blood workup and urine tests among others.

Treatment of neuropathy depends on the underlying cause such as vitamin B12 supplementation in case of B12 deficiency and diabetes control in diabetic neuropathy. Apart from this, symptomatic medications may be used in appropiate clinical setting. Physical therapy and rehabilitation is essential in case of weakness and imbalance.
