Movement disorders


Movement disorders are a group of illnesses characterised by abnormality in speed and coordination of movement. They can be divided into hypokinetic disorders, hyperkinetic disorders and ataxias.

Hypokinetic disorders are ones where there is a reduced speed of movement. Parkinson's disease, Vascular and drug induced Parkinsonism, PSP, MSA P are some examples of hypokinetic disorders.

Hyperkinetic disorders are those in which there is increased movement than normal, such as in tics, dystonia, dyskinesias, Huntington's chorea and other choreas. Movements may mimic dancing moves or snake like writhing or may be flinging of limbs depending on the cause.

Ataxias are characterised by imbalance. This may occur in abnormalities of the cerebellum located in the back of the head, or in disease of certain types of sensory nerves.

Evaluation usually entails brain scan and some blood tests depending on the nature of symptoms. Treatment options include oral medications, botox injections and surgical options such as deep brain stimulation in the appropriate setting. Physical therapy and rehabilitation is extremely important as well.
